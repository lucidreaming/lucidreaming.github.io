What is Dreaming?

Mental activity, who does it, interpretations

Dreaming consists of any mental activity, usually involuntary, that takes place during sleep. Such activity can involve images, sounds, ideas, emotions, and many other sensations. Dreams primarily occur during rapid eye movement (REM) sleep, when brain activity is high. Dreams may occur during other stages of sleep, but they are typically less vivid or memorable.

The nature of dreams varies greatly, as content can range from normal and ordinary to frightening and bizarre, or even sexual. The events in dreams are typically out of the dreamer’s control, with the exception of lucid dreaming, where the dreamer becomes self-aware.

Interpretations

The content and purpose of dreams are not definitively understood, though they have been a subject of scientific, as well as philosophical and religious, inquiry for centuries. The Greeks and Romans believed that dreams were directed messages from one or more deities, or from deceased persons, and that they predicted the future. Other prominent theories suggest that dreaming plays a substantial role in memory formation, problem solving, and gaining insight.

Most people today, instead, endorse Freud’s theory of dreams. Known as the father of psychoanalysis, Sigmund Freud posited that repressed wishes, emotions, fears, etc. from far back in the mind are released when one dreams. However, the content of these dreams must be censored, or at least distorted, because their disturbing nature would result in awakening or even degradation of self esteem.

Freud’s theory distinguishes between two types of content: ‘manifest content,’ what the dreamer subjectively experiences, and ‘latent content,’ or the deep-rooted meaning of a dream. In The Interpretation of Dreams (1899), Freud developed a psychological technique to interpret and understand the symbols and motifs present in dreams. Unfortunately, Freud claimed that only he was able to identify the underlying meaning of his patients’ dreams. Therefore, his theory is not testable and cannot be proven wrong.

“The virtuous man contents himself with dreaming that which the wicked man does in actual life.” - Sigmund Freud, The Interpretation of Dreams
